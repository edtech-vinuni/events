About This Seminar

We are pleased to invite you to this research seminar featuring Prof. Hisashi Hayashi from the Advanced Institute of Industrial Technology (AIIT) in Tokyo, Japan. This seminar explores the intersection of AI planning and large language models in creating collaborative decision support systems.

Abstract

Recent advances in large language models have opened new possibilities for agent-based decision support, where autonomous agents and multi-agent systems can reason, communicate, simulate possible situations, and support human decision making.

This talk introduces recent research activities on AI planning, multi-agent simulation, and LLM-based agents. It first provides a brief overview of agents, multi-agent systems, and LLM agents. It then discusses AI planning as a core technology for autonomous decision making, including examples from robot action planning and planning under legal and ethical constraints.

The talk then presents two case studies of LLM-based agents: an LLM-based framework for risk scenario generation and mitigation planning for AI systems, and a multi-agent conversation simulation approach for evacuation decision-making.

Through these case studies, the talk discusses how planning techniques, multi-agent simulation, and LLM agents can contribute to explainable and human-centered decision support in complex social and organizational contexts.
